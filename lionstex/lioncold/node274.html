prele (7882)

Unlock the ``inode'' and ``wake'' any waiting processes. This procedure is called by several others (especially ``iput''), in addition to ``readp'' and ``writep''. Section Five is the final section: last but not least. It is concerned with input/output for the slower, character oriented peripheral deviees.

Such devices share a common buffer pool, which is manipulated by a set of standard procedures.

The set of character oriented peripheral devices are exemplified by the following:

  • KL/DL11 interactive terminal
  • PC11 paper tape reader/punch
  • LP11 line printer.
